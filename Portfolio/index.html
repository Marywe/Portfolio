About me

 Hello World! My name is María "Marywe" López and I am a video game developer.


 I have been in the world of video games for five years and, although not all of my time has been doing projects, I have learned a lot along the way.


 My main role in the industry and the one I am most passionate about is in Quality Assurance. I worked for two years in a team without having a more specialized position than Lead QA, so we carried out all kinds of tasks.


 On the other hand, I have a small group called Kiwi Team with which I create video games and board games. The three of us handle all tasks, from programming to design, and we have a nice visual style.


 For as long as I can remember, I've been playing video games as a major hobby. At the age of 12, there was only one degree program in Spain dedicated to video game development, and that's when I knew I wanted to dedicate my life to it, even if it meant investing a lot of effort and time.


 Every day I strive to improve as a developer, learning new techniques and undertaking small projects to evolve. It's a field open to a huge world of possibilities, and I'm passionate about what I've managed to do: develop games.

Projects

 Throughout my professional career, I've worked on professional titles like the MMO Temtem (Cremagames) and developed different games with my little team, ranging from board games to casual mobile games, and many other genres, with my main role being a programmer. Moreover, we're dedicating a significant amount of time to one particular project, hoping it will make a notable impact. We're incredibly excited and can't wait to share some spoilers! You can find more of our work on my itch.io page.




  My work done in Temtem consisted in a lot of different things:

  • Identifying and reporting bugs.
  • Developing and passing monthly tests, unit tests and certification tests.
  • Working with dev kits and its respective software (PlayStation 5, Nintendo Switch, Xbox Series X and SteamDeck).
  • Using tools such as ping monitoring, tampering and server tools.
  • Reviewing people's problems in the forums and testing them.
  • Revising UIs, resolutions, graphics and audio options.
  • Even checking some texts for translation or localisation purposes.

 Another of my games to exhibit is Yo, Creatureto. A runner inspired by the dino game played in the program Yo, Interneto. A project done for Mahou in which you will play with the original characters of the program and fight to be the first in the leaderboard!
